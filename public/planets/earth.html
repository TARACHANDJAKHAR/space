Earth

The Blue Planet - Our Home in Space

Overview

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life. About 71% of Earth's surface is covered with water, making it appear blue from space. Earth's atmosphere is rich in nitrogen and oxygen, essential for most life forms.

Surface and Composition

Earth's surface is divided into several tectonic plates that move over time. The planet consists of a solid iron inner core, a liquid outer core, a mantle, and a crust. The surface features continents, oceans, mountains, valleys, and various other geological formations.

Atmosphere and Climate

Earth's atmosphere consists primarily of nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%). The atmosphere, along with the greenhouse effect, helps maintain temperatures suitable for life. Earth experiences various climate patterns and weather phenomena across different regions.

Life on Earth

Earth is unique in our solar system as the only known planet to support life. The planet hosts millions of species of living organisms, from microscopic bacteria to complex multicellular life forms. Humans have developed advanced civilizations and technologies, allowing us to study both our planet and the cosmos beyond.

Interesting Facts

Water Planet

Earth is the only planet known to have liquid water on its surface.

Active Geology

Earth has over 1,500 active volcanoes and constant tectonic plate movement.

Biodiversity

Earth hosts an estimated 8.7 million species of living organisms.
