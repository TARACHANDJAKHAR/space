Jupiter

The Giant Planet - King of the Solar System

Overview

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system and the fifth planet from the Sun. It is a gas giant with a mass more than two and a half times that of all the other planets in the solar system combined. Jupiter's iconic Great Red Spot is a giant storm that has been raging for at least 400 years.

Composition and Structure

Jupiter is primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, similar to the Sun. The planet likely has a small rocky core surrounded by a deep layer of metallic hydrogen, an outer layer of liquid hydrogen and helium, and an atmosphere of mostly hydrogen with traces of other elements.

Atmosphere and Weather

Jupiter's atmosphere features colorful bands and swirls, including its famous Great Red Spot, a giant storm system larger than Earth. The planet's rapid rotation creates strong atmospheric jet streams, separating its clouds into dark belts and bright zones.

Exploration History

Key missions to Jupiter include:

  • Pioneer 10 & 11 (1973, 1974): First flybys of Jupiter
  • Voyager 1 & 2 (1979): Detailed studies of Jupiter and its moons
  • Galileo (1995-2003): First spacecraft to orbit Jupiter
  • Juno (2016-present): Studying Jupiter's composition and magnetic field

Interesting Facts

Great Red Spot

Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a storm so large that three Earths could fit inside it.

Galilean Moons

Jupiter's four largest moons (Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto) were discovered by Galileo in 1610.

Magnetic Field

Jupiter has the strongest magnetic field of any planet in the solar system.
