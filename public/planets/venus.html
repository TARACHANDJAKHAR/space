Venus

Earth's Twin - The Morning Star

Overview

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often called Earth's sister planet due to their similar size and mass. Named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty, Venus is the brightest natural object in Earth's night sky after the Moon.

Surface and Composition

Venus has a solid surface covered with thousands of volcanoes and a thick atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide. The surface is obscured by an opaque layer of clouds made of sulfuric acid. The planet's extreme surface pressure and temperature make it one of the most hostile planetary environments in the Solar System.

Atmosphere and Climate

Venus has the densest atmosphere of all terrestrial planets, consisting mainly of carbon dioxide with clouds of sulfuric acid. This creates a runaway greenhouse effect, making Venus the hottest planet in our solar system with surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead.

Exploration History

Notable missions to Venus include:

  • Venera Program (1961-1984): First successful landing on another planet
  • Magellan (1989-1994): Mapped 98% of Venus's surface
  • Venus Express (2005-2014): Studied atmosphere and surface

Interesting Facts

Backward Rotation

Venus rotates on its axis backward, compared to most other planets.

Toxic Atmosphere

The atmospheric pressure on Venus's surface is 90 times that of Earth.

Volcanic Activity

Venus has more volcanoes than any other planet in the solar system.
