Mercury

The Swift Planet - Closest to the Sun

Overview

Mercury is the smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System. Named after the Roman god of commerce, Mercury completes an orbit around the Sun every 88 Earth days, the shortest of all the Sun's planets.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: 4,879 km
  • Day Length: 176 Earth days
  • Distance from Sun: 57.9 million km
  • Surface Temperature: -180°C to 430°C
  • Number of Moons: 0
  • Orbital Period: 88 Earth days

Surface and Composition

Mercury's surface resembles that of Earth's Moon, bearing witness to a history of heavy bombardment by meteoroids and comets. The planet's surface is covered with craters, ridges, and smooth plains. The largest crater, the Caloris Basin, spans 1,550 kilometers in diameter.

Atmosphere and Climate

Mercury has a very thin atmosphere, often called an exosphere, composed mostly of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium. Due to its proximity to the Sun and lack of substantial atmosphere, Mercury experiences extreme temperature variations, from scorching hot during the day to freezing cold at night.

Exploration History

Only two space missions have studied Mercury up close:

  • Mariner 10 (1974-1975): First spacecraft to visit Mercury
  • MESSENGER (2011-2015): First spacecraft to orbit Mercury

Interesting Facts

Extreme Temperatures

Mercury experiences the most extreme temperature variations of any planet in our solar system.

Unusual Rotation

Mercury rotates three times for every two orbits around the Sun, creating a unique day-night cycle.

Dense Core

Despite its small size, Mercury has the second highest density of all planets due to its large iron core.
