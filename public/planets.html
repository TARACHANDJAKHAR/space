Our Solar System

Explore the planets that orbit our Sun, from the rocky inner worlds to the gas giants of the outer system.

The Planets

Click on a planet to learn more about it

Mercury

The Swift Planet

  • Diameter: 4,879 km
  • Day Length: 176 Earth days
  • Surface Temp: -180°C to 430°C

The smallest and innermost planet in our Solar System, Mercury is a rocky world with extreme temperatures.

Venus

Earth's Twin

  • Diameter: 12,104 km
  • Day Length: 243 Earth days
  • Surface Temp: 462°C

Often called Earth's sister planet, Venus has a thick atmosphere and is the hottest planet in our solar system.

Earth

The Blue Planet

  • Diameter: 12,742 km
  • Day Length: 24 hours
  • Average Temp: 15°C

Our home planet, Earth is the only known world to support life, with liquid water and a protective atmosphere.

Mars

The Red Planet

  • Diameter: 6,779 km
  • Day Length: 24.6 hours
  • Average Temp: -63°C

The red planet Mars has long fascinated humans, with its potential for past or present microbial life.

Jupiter

The Giant Planet

  • Diameter: 139,820 km
  • Day Length: 10 hours
  • Moons: 79 known

The largest planet in our solar system, Jupiter is a gas giant with a Great Red Spot and numerous moons.

Saturn

The Ringed Planet

  • Diameter: 116,460 km
  • Day Length: 10.7 hours
  • Ring System: 282,000 km wide

Famous for its spectacular ring system, Saturn is a gas giant with a complex system of moons.

Uranus

The Sideways Planet

  • Diameter: 50,724 km
  • Day Length: 17.2 hours
  • Average Temp: -224°C

Unique among planets, Uranus rotates on its side and has a blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere.

Neptune

The Windy Planet

  • Diameter: 49,244 km
  • Day Length: 16.1 hours
  • Wind Speeds: Up to 2,100 km/h

The most distant planet, Neptune is known for its deep blue color and extreme wind speeds.

Pluto

The Dwarf Planet

  • Diameter: 2,377 km
  • Day Length: 6.4 Earth days
  • Average Temp: -232°C

Pluto is composed of about 70% rock and 30% ice. Its surface features a variety of terrains, including mountains, valleys, plains, and craters. The most notable feature is the heart-shaped Tombaugh Regio, which is made of frozen nitrogen, methane, and carbon monoxide.

Did You Know?

Fascinating facts about our solar system

Solar System Age

Our solar system formed about 4.6 billion years ago.

Asteroid Belt

Millions of asteroids orbit between Mars and Jupiter.

Temperature Range

Temperatures range from 462°C on Venus to -224°C on Uranus.
